I started participating in mathematics and informatics competitions from an early age, which has been a defining aspect of my academic life. Between 2013 and 2019, I qualified every year for the national phase of the Mathematics Olympiad and participated as well to multiple international and national competitions, while from 2014 to 2019, I was also a consistent participant in the national phase of the Informatics Olympiad. For a brief period during 2015-2016, I also explored physics competitions, qualifying for the national phase of the Physics Olympiad. However, I ultimately decided to focus my efforts on mathematics and informatics.

My participation in competitions came to a halt in 2020 when the COVID-19 pandemic disrupted most events. In 2021, I graduated from high school and transitioned my focus towards machine learning and AI, aiming to apply my skills to develop innovative solutions for real-worlds problems.
