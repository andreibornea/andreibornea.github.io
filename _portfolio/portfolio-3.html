The 2D Fluid Simulation project demonstrates realistic fluid dynamics using incompressible Euler equations, Voronoï diagrams, and LBFGS optimization for visually compelling animations. This project showcases advanced computational techniques to simulate and visualize fluid behavior with high precision.

Features

  • Incompressible Euler Equations: Implements de Gallouet-Mérigot's scheme for realistic and stable fluid motion.
  • Voronoï and Power Diagrams: Employs spatial partitioning to model fluid cells and their interactions.
  • LBFGS Optimization: Ensures incompressibility by optimizing weights for power diagrams during simulation.
  • Realistic Animations: Generates visually engaging fluid animations showcasing particle motion.
